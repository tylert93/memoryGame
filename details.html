Overview

The aim of the memory game is to get rid of all the cards on the screen. The player is allowed to flip over 2 cards at a time, revealing the image underneath. If the two cards selected by the player have the same image, both cards are removed. If not, both cards are flipped back over. The player must remember the individual images under each card in order to pair them all correctly. There are two of each card in the whole deck.
An array called ’cardArray’ is created, each object in which represents a card in the deck. Each object is given a ‘name’ and ‘img’ attribute. There are two of each card in the deck, with identical attributes. The Durstenfeld shuffle algorithm is used to randomise the order of the deck at the start of every game.
At the start of the game, a ‘for loop’ and the ‘.setAttribute()’ method is used to create card elements on the screen, set the background image of every card to be the back of a playing card, making the card appear facedown and also give each card an ‘id’ attribute equal to their index. Whenever a card is clicked, its background image is changed to the ‘img’ attribute of the object in the cardArray with the same index. The ‘.push()’ method is used to place the ‘name’ attribute of the card into a new array called ‘cardsChosen’.
Once a second card has been flipped over, both items in the ‘cardsChosen’ array will be compared. If the two are equal, the player has found a match and the cards are removed from the screen. The ‘cardsChosen’ array will be placed in another array called ‘cardsWon’. If the cards do not match then both cards are flipped back over. Once the length of the ‘cardsWon’ array is half the length of the ‘cardsArray’ then the player will have found all the pairs of cards.
